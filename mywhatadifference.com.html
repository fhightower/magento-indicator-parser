Non-Controversial Occlusion
The Truth.
Each practitioner has a moral and ethical obligation to try to discover the truth. Could there be a truth regarding occlusion? We have all been led to believe that a real truth could not and does not exist. It's a documented fact that dental professionals have been searching for this truth for well over one hundred fifty years. It has never been found. What has been found is controversy.

In simple terms, the truth would have to be flawless dentistry.

It would have to be non-controversial and completely principle based. There could never be a controversy when perfection is realized. Furthermore, the truth gives us a defined reason for everything we encounter. It becomes our consistent guide to "performing", not "practicing" successful dentistry.

When you think about it, if in fact this truth does exist and you presently practice conventionally or under the guidelines of a philosophy...well, isn't this shortchanging your patient? Isn't this shortchanging yourself? Most of all, isn't this a misrepresentation of the profession?

To make the claim, "We have found the truth" would in fact place the individual making this statement on a pedestal open to criticism from every existing philosophy based camp in the nation.

Usually, when a new idea surfaces, there are continuous comparisons to the current literature, followed by a barrage of criticism. The idea slowly loses its validity while under this microscope. The new concept which is "principle based" always contradicts some established, published literature which is also "principle based." The resulting studies pick apart the new idea and the pedestal begins to crumble.
