HDMI Cables
HDMI cables is the present standard video and audio connection for your high definition home entertainment. New high definition television these days now have at the very least two HDMI input and similarly in electronic appliances like Blu ray players, DVDs, game consoles, DVD player. Computers have HDMI outputs for video and audio. HDMI is “High Definition Multimedia Interface”; it can transmit high definition audio and high definition video in just one cable. Connecting HDMI is simple; you connect the one end of the cable on the electronic device’s HDMI output slot, while you connect the other end to the HDTV’s HDMI input slot. That’s less clutter, a high definition experience in just a single cable. There’s no reason to spend more on your high definition requirements, you can get the best and most affordable HDMI cables only here in Cable Selector.  
Audio cables
Audio cables is required if you need to connect two equipment together or you need to join the audio circuit to wiring controls. Audio cables protect the sound signals, shielding out any external electromagnetic interferences. A common impedance are electrical fields coming from 230v AC, with the mains cable within the walls, those leading into the socket, will transmit a 50Hz hum sound. The hum is usually picked up by the lead connector in sensitive audio circuits of amplifiers. Simply, audio cables are wires protected by woven shield, twisted foil, curled wires or outer layers. The audio cable picks up the undesired hum, before it touches your desired audio signal. Audio cables are often paired with video cables. Video cables
Video cables refer to the type of cables that transmits colour and brightness in a separate wire. The use of video cables often results to a much higher video quality, better than composite video cables which carry two signals in one cable. Video cables are often paired with audio cables. Generating video signals with video cables on a monitor or display device is simple and clear cut. Electronic devices like Blu Ray players, Xbox 360, Playstation3 and DVD players stores, processes and transfers video signals with varying methods, but with only a single video signal option. It maintains clear signals by isolating the video signal components so it will not interrupt with another. This type of separated signal is created in ‘composite video’ like RGB, YPbPr and S Video.
